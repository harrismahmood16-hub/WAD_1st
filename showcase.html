Project A — Restaurant Digital Transformation (Problem-Solving Page)

Role: CTO / Lead Developer (project owner)

Problem addressed: The restaurant relied on phone calls and paper notes for bookings and feedback, causing missed reservations and slow customer response. No social presence or easy contact options existed.

Solution & outcome: I designed and implemented a lightweight website with clear contact details, social links, and an interactive booking/feedback form that generates an email directly to restaurant staff. This cut manual handling time and provided a reliable electronic trail for bookings. The site prioritised accessibility and progressive enhancement so it works on low-end devices and smart phones.

Design concept for the Problem-Solving Page: The Problem-Solving Page was structured to follow the user journey: discover (social links & contact details), act (clear booking form), and confirm (auto-generated email via mailto). I used minimal fields to reduce friction (name, contact, request), accessible labels, and client-side validation. The mailto approach was chosen because the assessment brief forbids live databases — it provides immediate, actionable messages sent to staff without requiring server-side code. The page emphasises clarity and speed to encourage bookings and feedback, improving customer engagement with minimal technical overhead.

Project B — Online Menu & Specials

Role: Front-end developer

Problem: Customers couldn't view the latest menu or daily specials online, resulting in missed opportunities to upsell seasonal dishes.

Outcome: Built a responsive menu page and a small admin-editable JSON feed for menu items. The restaurant reported higher enquiries about specials and saw an increase in walk-in orders for items promoted online.

Project C — Staff Scheduling Dashboard (Group Assignment)

Role: Team developer (group project)

Problem: Manual scheduling led to clashes and inefficient shift allocation.

Outcome: Created a simple JavaScript-driven scheduling UI to visualise shifts and export schedules as CSV. The prototype reduced scheduling conflicts in the test group and formed the basis for further work.
