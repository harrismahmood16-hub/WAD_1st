Restaurant Digital Transformation — Case Study

Business Context & Goals

The restaurant relied on manual phone bookings and paper notes, resulting in missed reservations, delayed feedback, and poor customer engagement. The goal is a web-based solution that improves efficiency, streamlines bookings, and integrates social media to attract new customers.

Objectives include: 1) faster booking handling, 2) easier customer communication, 3) professional online presence, and 4) accessible design for all devices.

Implemented Features

  • Social media integration: links to Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X
  • Clear display of restaurant contact info (email, phone)
  • Interactive booking/feedback form that generates a pre-filled email
  • Mobile-first and accessible design
  • Minimalistic interface to reduce user friction

Detailed Problem-Solving Approach

User Journey: The page is structured to guide users from discovery (social & contact info), to action (booking form), to confirmation (auto-email). This improves engagement and reduces errors.

Form Design: Fields include name, email, date/time, number of people, and special requests. Client-side validation ensures required fields are completed. The form uses a mailto: link to open the user's email client, ensuring compliance with assessment brief (no server/database).

Visual Design: Clean layout with blue-highlighted text boxes for emphasis, card-style sections for readability, and consistent spacing. The design ensures clarity and reduces cognitive load for users.

Booking / Feedback Form

How it works: The form builds a mailto URL and opens your email client with a pre-filled message. This eliminates the need for server-side infrastructure while providing a real-world workflow.
